Bermuda

Bermuda is a British Overseas Territory in the North Atlantic Ocean. It is known for its pink-sand beaches such as Elbow Beach and Horseshoe Bay. The massive Royal Naval Dockyard complex combines modern attractions like the interactive Dolphin Quest with maritime history at the National Museum of Bermuda. The island has a distinctive blend of British and American culture, which can be found in the capital, Hamilton.

Okinawa

Okinawa is a Japanese prefecture comprising more than 150 islands in the East China Sea between Taiwan and Japan's mainland. It's known for its tropical climate, broad beaches, and coral reefs, as well as its World War II sites. The busy capital of Naha and Churaumi Aquarium, home to whale sharks and manta rays, lie on the largest island (also named Okinawa). The island is also known for its Shuri Castle, a restored royal palace from the Ryukyu Kingdom.

Capas, Tarlac

Capas is a first-class municipality in the province of Tarlac in the Philippines. It is known for being the location of the Capas National Shrine, a national shrine of the Philippines that commemorates the Filipino and American soldiers who died in Camp O'Donnell at the end of the Bataan Death March during World War II. The shrine is located near Mount Samat in the nearby municipality of Pilar.

Pohang-si

Pohang is a city in the province of North Gyeongsang, South Korea, and a main seaport in the Daegu-Gyeongbuk region. The built-up area of Pohang is located on the north side of the Hyeongsan River. The city is divided into two wards (gu), Buk-gu or Northern Ward and Nam-gu or Southern Ward. This city has a number of tourist attractions, including the Jukdo Market, Homigot Sunrise Square, and the Pohang Museum of Steel Art.
